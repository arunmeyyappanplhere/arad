Available Traditional Rescue Methods

Avalanche Transceivers Beacons


These are wearable devices that continuously emit and receive radio signals. In an avalanche, each person with a transceiver can switch to “search” mode to locate the signal of a buried victim quickly.

Avalanche Airbag Systems


Integrated into a backpack, these systems inflate rapidly during an avalanche to help keep the wearer near the surface of the snow, reducing the likelihood of burial and increasing survival chances.

Avalanche Probes and Shovels


Often used in conjunction with transceivers, probes help pinpoint the exact location of a buried victim, while shovels are used to quickly excavate the snow, significantly shortening rescue time.

Rescue Dogs


Highly trained avalanche rescue dogs can detect human scent beneath layers of snow, providing critical assistance in locating victims where electronic devices or visual methods might struggle.

How is ARAD better than any other?

Traditional Rescue

  • Manual SOS Activation Required: Alerts rescuers only when manually triggered.
  • No Real-Time GPS Tracking: Location details are not continuously shared, potentially delaying rescue.
  • No Buzzer & LED Alerts: User isn't notified before SOS activation.
  • Heavy & Non-Waterproof: Not suitable for extreme weather conditions.
  • Limited Coverage in Remote Areas: May not function reliably in isolated locations.
  • Short Battery Life: Might run out during extended treks.

ARAD Rescue

  • Automated SOS Activation: No manual intervention needed for alerting rescuers.
  • Real-Time GPS Tracking: Sends precise location details for quick rescue.
  • Buzzer & LED Alerts: Notifies the user before SOS activation.
  • Lightweight & Waterproof: Designed for extreme weather conditions.
  • Works in Remote Areas: Uses GSM technology for emergency communication.
  • Long Battery Life: Ensures prolonged operation during long treks.
