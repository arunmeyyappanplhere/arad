NUMBER OF PEOPLE GOT SAVED PROJECT: ARAD

Kate Winslet talks about her motivation for taking on the challenge of a lifetime on Running Wild With Bear Grylls The actress goes on a grueling two-day adventure with Grylls in the treacherous but beautiful mountains of Snowdonia

Emma, the Solo Snowboarder Emma, an adventurous solo snowboarder, was carving through fresh powder on a remote mountain slope when disaster struck. A sudden crack echoed, and the snow beneath her feet gave way. Within seconds, she was engulfed in a roaring white wave. Buried under several feet of snow, panic threatened to take over, but she remembered her ARAD device. The rescue team received her signal within minutes. Guided by the precise location beacon, they reached her just in time. Shivering but alive, Emma credits ARAD for her second chance at life.

Lucas, the Mountain Guide Lucas, a seasoned mountain guide, always prioritized safety. Leading a group of novice hikers, he ensured everyone was equipped with an ARAD device. Halfway through the trek, an unexpected avalanche swept them off the trail. Lucas was buried but stayed calm, knowing his ARAD would transmit his exact location. The rescue team located him and his group quickly, thanks to the interconnected signals from each ARAD device. Lucas now insists on ARAD for every expedition, calling it his "silent guardian."

Amara, the Ski Enthusiast Amara lived for the thrill of off-piste skiing. But one fateful day, an avalanche caught her off-guard. She was dragged down the slope and trapped under heavy snow. Her ARAD device automatically activated, sending out a distress signal. Her skiing partner received the signal on his receiver and was able to dig her out within minutes. Amara reflects on the incident with gratitude, realizing how close she was to a tragic end. She now advocates for ARAD, calling it the most vital piece of gear for any skier.
